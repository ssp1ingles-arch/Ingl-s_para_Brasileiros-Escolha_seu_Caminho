Sistema 09 · gramática sob medida

Dúvidas Pontuais

Os pontos específicos que confundem o brasileiro, cada um numa ficha de referência rápida: a dúvida, a regra em uma linha, três exemplos corretos e o erro clássico para nunca mais repetir.

8dúvidas
A1 · A2foco
Referênciarápida
01A1

DID + verbo base

RegraQuando DID/DIDN'T aparece como auxiliar, ele já carrega o passado — o verbo principal volta à forma base.
✅ Exemplos corretos
  • Did you go to school?
  • I didn't see it.
  • Did she like the gift?
Erro clássicoDid you went? → Did you go?
02A1

WENT vs DID + GO

RegraWENT só aparece na frase afirmativa. Em perguntas e negações, o passado vira trabalho do DID e o GO volta à base.
✅ Exemplos corretos
  • I went to the party.
  • I didn't go to the party.
  • Did you go to the party?
Erro clássicoI didn't went → I didn't go
03A2

WENT vs HAVE GONE

RegraWENT = ação terminada (com last week, yesterday). HAVE/HAS GONE = a pessoa foi e ainda está lá, com relevância no presente.
✅ Exemplos corretos
  • She went to London last week.
  • She's gone to London. (ainda está lá)
  • He went out an hour ago.
Erro clássicoHe's gone out an hour ago → He went out (tempo terminado)
04A1

Verbos irregulares: sem -ed

RegraVerbos irregulares não formam o passado com -ed — têm uma forma própria que precisa ser memorizada.
✅ Exemplos corretos
  • go → went (I went)
  • write → wrote (I wrote)
  • tell → told (I told)
Erro clássicoI goed / writed → I went / wrote
05A1

3ª pessoa no presente (-s)

RegraNo Simple Present, com he / she / it o verbo ganha -s no final. É o erro nº1 do brasileiro.
✅ Exemplos corretos
  • She cooks dinner every day.
  • He plays the guitar well.
  • It rains a lot in July.
Erro clássicoHe play → He plays
06A1

don't vs doesn't

RegraNo negativo do Simple Present: he / she / it usa DOESN'T; I / you / we / they usa DON'T. Depois, o verbo fica na base.
✅ Exemplos corretos
  • She doesn't like pizza.
  • He doesn't work here.
  • They don't agree.
Erro clássicoShe don't like → She doesn't like
07A2

for vs since

RegraFOR + período de duração (three years, two months). SINCE + ponto de início (2020, Monday).
✅ Exemplos corretos
  • I've lived here for three years.
  • She's worked here since 2020.
  • We've waited since Monday.
Erro clássicohas lived here since three years → for three years
08A1

There is vs There are

RegraTHERE IS + singular (um). THERE ARE + plural (dois ou mais). "Am" só vai com I.
✅ Exemplos corretos
  • There is a park near my house.
  • There are two cats on the sofa.
  • There is some water in the glass.
Erro clássicoThere is two cats → There are two cats

Novos cards são adicionados conforme surgem novas dúvidas: WAS vs WERE · HAVE vs HAS · CAN vs COULD · a / an / the.

Vocabulário Literário · The Birthday Party

SISTEMA 21 — THE BIRTHDAY PARTY: VOCABULÁRIO LITERÁRIO EM CONTEXTO

A HISTÓRIA COMPLETA (para exibir no sistema)

A couple at a restaurant. The man: self-satisfied, conspicuous in a dinner jacket. The woman: unmistakably his wife — fadingly pretty in a new hat with a gay big brim.
She had arranged a birthday surprise: a small cake with a single pink candle, the head waiter carrying it in, the violin and piano playing Happy Birthday.
She beamed with shy pride over her little surprise.
Such few people as there were in the restaurant tried to help out with a pattering of applause.
It became clear at once that help was needed. The husband was not pleased. He was hotly embarrassed and indignant.
When the orchestra finished and the attention of the room had shifted away from them, I saw him say something to her under his breath — some punishing thing, quick and curt and unkind.
I couldn't bear to look at the woman then. I stared at my plate and waited for quite a long time. Not long enough though.
Because she was still crying when I finally glanced over there again — crying quietly and heartbrokenly and hopelessly, all to herself, under the gay big brim of her best hat.

EXPRESSÕES DE IDADE — EARLY / MID / LATE + DÉCADA

Luke ensina este padrão nativo ao descrever as personagens:
