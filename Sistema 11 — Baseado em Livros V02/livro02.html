Nouns and adjectives

Ponto 1The articles a and the

  • I've got a cat.
  • fifty pence a kilo · four times a day · two hundred kilometres an hour
  • a hundred · a thousand · a couple · a dozen
  • I've just washed the car. (= our car / this car)
  • Where's the dog? · We've made the beds.
  • Can you play the guitar? · I like listening to the piano. · I've just bought a guitar and a piano.
  • Life isn't always easy. · Latin is a difficult language to learn.
  • Gold is very expensive at the moment. · They were looking for gold. · They found the gold.
  • They live in Manchester. · We went to church. · She's at home.

Ponto 2Words using a, the, or no article

  • I put the glass on the table. (definido)
  • I picked up a glass. (indefinido)
  • It's made of glass. (incontável)

Ponto 4Possessives with 's, s' and of

  • Monica's dress · the dog's nose · the children's school
  • dolphins' brains · the sailors' hats
  • the end of the road · the smell of cooking · the leader of the party
  • car keys · the kitchen door · a bus driver
  • a fortnight's holiday · two weeks' rest

Ponto 7Qualification of comparatives and superlatives

  • The chair is a bit / a little / much / a lot cheaper than that one.
  • This chair is as / nearly as / not as / twice as cheap as that one.
  • It's by far the most expensive car in the world.
  • It's easily the most expensive of all the cars I've seen.

Ponto 8so and such

  • She was such a good actress. · It's such a cold day, isn't it?
  • He shouldn't buy such expensive clothes.
  • You look so lovely! · He dresses so badly.
  • It's such a stupid idea that I don't think we should consider it.
  • I was so tired that I fell asleep in the lecture.

Verbs — the present tense

Ponto 9Present Simple and Present Continuous

  • She comes from Argentina. · I don't speak Chinese.
  • How often do you go to the gym? · He usually leaves work at 6 o'clock.
  • Are you waiting for the bus? · I'm writing a letter to Julie.

Ponto 10Verbs using Present Simple rather than Present Continuous

  • I believe you. · I don't understand. · What do you think?
  • I don't like him. · How do you feel? · I want to go.
  • Be quiet, please: I'm thinking. (atividade)
  • I'm seeing the president tomorrow. (see = encontrar-se)
  • I'm listening to the radio. (listen/watch/look = deliberado; hear/see = não)

Ponto 11Permanent and temporary

  • I live in Mexico, though I'm staying in Texas at the moment.
  • I'm living in Paris just now, but I come from Belgium.

Verbs — the past

Ponto 12Past Simple and Past Continuous

  • She played. · Did she play? · She didn't play.
  • I saw a great film last night. · We lived in Sydney for two years.
  • At 8 o'clock last night we were having dinner.
  • He was walking down the road when he heard a loud noise.

Ponto 13Past continuous as narrative

  • It was a typical summer afternoon: the sun was beating down, the cars were creeping slowly round the corner of the park.
  • Five or six children were playing in the stream by the fountain.
  • Walter Harrison, sitting on a park bench in his overcoat, was feeling cold and lonely.

Ponto 14Present Perfect

  • He's been here for 10 minutes. · We've lived here since 1996.
  • I've written three letters today. · She's travelled to six countries since she started the job.
  • I've broken a glass. · John has lost his job. · I've just heard the news.
  • Have you finished that report yet? · Have you ever been to China?
  • She's never flown in a plane. · She's the best teacher I've ever had.
  • I'm afraid I can't. Isabel's borrowed my bike for the weekend.
  • I'm sorry. He's just left the office.
  • He's hurt his back so he's gone to see the doctor.
  • It's the most beautiful country I've ever been to.
  • That's because we've walked 30 kilometres today.
  • Don't worry. I've already done it.
  • This is the first time I've driven this car.
  • I don't know. I haven't read it yet.
  • They've lived here for ten years. · I've cut my finger.

Ponto 16Present Perfect Continuous

  • I've run six kilometres. (simples — o resultado importa)
  • I've been running. (contínuo — a atividade importa)

Ponto 17Past Perfect

  • I had spoken to Mr Johnson before the meeting began.
  • By 1984, we had waited seven years for an answer.
  • I got to the stadium at 7.15, and the game started at 7.30. (o jogo começou DEPOIS que cheguei)
  • I got to the stadium at 7.45, and the game had started at 7.30. (o jogo começou ANTES)
  • When the police arrived, the car had gone.

Ponto 18Past Perfect Continuous

  • When I got there, she had been working all night.
  • They didn't like the house, even though they had been living there for years.
  • There were books everywhere: she had been reading for hours.

Verbs — the future

Ponto 20Present Continuous, going to, and Future Simple

  • I'm seeing Jessica tonight. (present continuous = futuro planejado)
  • They're going to open the new offices on 1st March.
  • Look at the snow. It's going to be difficult to get into work tomorrow.
  • Richard's going to be the new director.
  • The job will be finished by the weekend.
  • There's Eleanor. I'll go and talk to her. · I'll help you.

Ponto 21Present simple as future (timetables)

  • 'What time does our train leave?' 'The train leaves at eight, we arrive at Dover at twelve, and get on the ferry an hour later.'
  • Your plane leaves London at 8.30 and arrives in Cairo at 12.00.
  • The hovercraft leaves Felixstowe at 12.00. It takes an hour.

Ponto 22Future Continuous

  • I'll be waiting at the station when you arrive. · I'll be watching football at six.
  • I'll be living in London for the next few weeks.
  • I'll be seeing him tomorrow – I'll give him your note.
  • I'll be working at home tomorrow – you can call me there.
  • I'm going to see her tomorrow – we arranged it last week.
  • Peter and Tom will be lying on the beach in Corfu at the moment.
  • I bet they'll be having a good time now that it's hot.
  • They'll probably be staying at an expensive hotel somewhere.

Ponto 23Future Perfect

  • I'll have gone by 4 o'clock. · I'll have finished work by five o'clock tonight.
  • I'll have been here for a year in January.
  • We'll have been living here for two years in July.
  • I'll be dirty because I'll have been playing football.

Ponto 25Future in the past

  • I was going to leave early, but I didn't.
  • I was going to visit my parents yesterday, but my car broke down.
  • He was going to say something, but changed his mind.
  • They were going to stay in the Grand Hotel, but it was full – so they stayed with us instead.
  • I was going to go to university, but I failed my exams, so I left school at sixteen.

Simple, continuous and perfect aspects

Ponto 27Simple and continuous aspects

  • I'll have a bath tonight. · I worked from eight o'clock to ten o'clock.
  • I go to the office by car. · Big cars use a lot of petrol.
  • Classes start at nine o'clock. · We went to Spain for our holiday.
  • I understood the lecture quite well.
  • They're working at the moment. · I was reading when you phoned.
  • At this time tomorrow we'll be saying goodbye.
  • I'm cycling to work this week because my car's in the garage.
  • I'm living in London at the moment.

Ponto 28Perfect aspect

  • I have been to France twice.
  • It was 1946. The war had finished and Jack had left the army.
  • Come tomorrow at 7.30. I'll have had dinner by then.

Verb formations

Ponto 30Verbs taking gerund, infinitive, or indirect speech

  • She advised me to go. (verbo + infinitivo)
  • I like driving. (verbo + gerúndio)
  • They complained that they had been waiting for hours. (discurso indireto)
  • They accused me of taking the money. · I apologized for being late.
  • We all helped to move the car. · I intend to stay here for some time.
  • We invited them to come to the party. · Why didn't they let him go?
  • They made him stay. · We finally managed to open the car door.
  • Do you mind my smoking? · Do you mind if I smoke?
  • They offered to pay for the damage. · They refused to leave.
  • They promised to visit her. · They promised that they would visit her.
  • I remembered to pick up my keys. · They told me to leave early.
  • She suggested going to a different school. · She suggested that we should go.
  • Please try to speak more quietly. · I warned him to be careful.
  • It seems that they aren't coming.

The passive

Ponto 31The passive: Present / Past / Present Perfect

  • Active: Simon has painted the house. → Passive: The house has been painted.
  • The cars are taken abroad. · The cars were taken abroad. · The cars have been taken abroad.
  • The garages are cleaned every day.
  • He has been given a lot of money. · Two hundred people were arrested.

Ponto 32The passive: all tenses, simple and continuous

  • The letters are delivered here. (present simple)
  • He's being interviewed at the moment. (present continuous)
  • They were sent by train. (past simple)
  • My suit was being cleaned at the time. (past continuous)
  • All the tickets have been sold. (present perfect)
  • I was too late: the papers had been removed. (past perfect)
  • You will be met at the station. (futuro)
  • I don't want to be arrested. · We shouldn't be arrested. (infinitivo)
  • He doesn't like being told what to do. (gerúndio)
  • The cars are washed, cleaned, checked, and then driven to the ports.

Ponto 33to have something done

  • James has his car cleaned every week.
  • I had my suit cleaned last week.
  • She's going to have her house repaired by the Council.
  • I'm having my house painted at the moment.
  • I've had the car fixed. · I'll have your coat cleaned for you.
  • He had his car cleaned. (≠ He had cleaned his car — a ordem muda o sentido)
  • I had my bag stolen last week. (uso coloquial)
  • We have the newspapers delivered.

Miscellaneous — wish

Ponto 34wish (+ past simple / past perfect / would)

  • I wish things were different now.
  • I wish I was rich. / I wish I were rich.
  • I wish I lived in America. · Do you wish we lived nearer the school?
  • I wish I didn't have to go to school on Saturdays.
  • I wish things had been different in the past.
  • I wish I hadn't stolen that money. · I wish she had come to my party.
  • I wish we'd gone to Alan's party.
  • I wish things would change soon. · I wish he would give up smoking.
  • I wish it would stop raining. · I wish she would be more careful.
  • I wish John would come home before twelve o'clock.
  • If only I was rich. · If only I hadn't stolen that money. · If only it would stop raining.

Conditionals

Ponto 36First, second and zero conditionals

  • If it's hot tomorrow, we'll go to the beach. · We'll go to the beach if it's hot. (1st)
  • If I was rich, I could buy a yacht. · I could buy a yacht if I was rich. (2nd — não sou rico)
  • If Peter got that job, we'd move to Edinburgh. (2nd)
  • If I go to bed late, I always feel tired the next day. (zero)
  • If you cook vegetables for too long, they taste awful. (zero)

Ponto 37The third conditional

  • If I had met you earlier, I wouldn't have married Joe.
  • If I'd worked harder, I would have passed the exam.
  • I'd have warned you if I'd seen you last week. (mas não te vi)
  • If I'd passed my exams, I would have gone to Art College.
  • What would you have done if I hadn't lent you the money?
  • If you had asked me for tickets, I could have got you some.

Ponto 38Words other than if (unless, provided that, suppose)

  • I'll go home soon if the film doesn't start. = I'll go home soon unless the film starts.
  • Provided that everybody agrees, we'll have the meeting on Tuesday.
  • Just suppose it didn't rain for four months: would we have enough water?

Ponto 39Sentences containing hidden conditions

  • 'James didn't come to the party.' 'What a pity. I'm sure he would have enjoyed it.' (se tivesse vindo)
  • Be careful, Tom. I wouldn't hurry this decision. (se eu fosse você)
  • You should have come. You would have loved it. (se você tivesse vindo)

Ponto 40Mixed tense conditionals

  • If I'd stayed at school, I would be in university now. (passado → consequência presente)
  • If you hear the alarm, get out as fast as you can. (condição + imperativo)
  • I'll take your plate if you've finished.
  • If he saw us, he always said hello. (zero conditional no passado)

Modals

Ponto 42Modals — introduction

  • 12 modais: can, could, shall, should, must, ought to, may, might, will, would, need (to), dare.
  • We should stay. · We shouldn't stay. · Should we stay? · Shouldn't we stay?

Ponto 43can, could, be able to

  • I could drive when I was eighteen. (= sabia dirigir)
  • I was able to drive to college because my uncle lent me a car for the year. (sabia E fiz)
  • I'll be able to see you tomorrow. / I can see you tomorrow.
  • It was too expensive – I couldn't buy it. / I wasn't able to buy it.

Ponto 44must, have to: positive and negative

  • You must leave. (= you can't stay) · You have to leave.
  • You mustn't leave. (proibição)
  • You don't have to leave. (sem obrigação — pode ficar ou ir)
  • I've got to go. = I have to go.

Ponto 45must, have to: tense changes

  • I must do this tomorrow morning. (must + tempo = futuro)
  • You mustn't leave. · You don't have to leave. · Must you leave? · Do you have to leave?
  • I had to leave the party early last night – I wasn't very well.

Ponto 46should, ought to

  • We ought to stay here. / We should stay here.
  • Ought we to go? / Should we go?
  • You shouldn't make a noise in here. / You oughtn't to make a noise in here.
  • You must go now! (sem escolha) / You should go now. (sua decisão)
  • Oughtn't you to go home now?
  • They should be in bed. / They ought to be in bed.

Ponto 47should, ought to, must, have to

  • You should tell your father about this problem. / You ought to tell your father about this problem.
  • They should be here soon. / They ought to be here soon.
  • I asked if I should open the door. (discurso indireto)
  • Parent to child: 'You must be back by seven o'clock.' (autoridade do falante)
  • One friend to another: 'You'll have to get up early to catch the morning train.' (obrigação da situação)

Ponto 48need + infinitive

  • I need to practise my English.
  • You don't need to have a visa to go to France from Britain.
  • Teacher to students: 'You needn't do any homework tonight.'
  • Do I need to apply for a visa? · Need I do any homework this weekend?

Ponto 49need as a main verb and need + gerund

  • I need some petrol. · Do you need anything else?
  • My car needs cleaning. (= My car needs to be cleaned.)
  • Your hair needs washing. · Your house needs painting.

Ponto 50need in the past

  • I needed something to eat. · The house needed painting.
  • I didn't need to get up early yesterday. (não era necessário, então não fiz)
  • I needn't have got up early yesterday. (não era necessário, mas fiz)
  • I didn't need to catch the bus this morning, because Vic gave me a lift.
  • I needn't have lent him that money.

Ponto 52Modals in the past tense

  • I should have known. · He might have arrived. · They ought to have gone.
  • Should I have known? · Ought they to have gone? · Could he have arrived?
  • I shouldn't have gone. · He couldn't have known. · They oughtn't to have gone.
  • You shouldn't have said that. · They might not have gone.
  • I needn't have fixed my bike yesterday – I could have used my father's.

Ponto 53Modals in the past: deduction

  • You shouldn't have done that. · You ought to have warned me about him.
  • John might have told Mrs Jones what happened. · Sheila may have left two or three days ago.
  • Mike could have stayed in this hotel. (= é possível)
  • Mike must have stayed in this hotel. (= quase certo que sim)
  • Mike can't have stayed in this hotel. (= quase certo que não)
  • I could have left at ten o'clock, but I didn't.
  • He's late – something must be wrong! (dedução no presente)
  • How did you know about the wedding? Someone must have told you!

Ponto 54could, would, might

  • I could do it if you wanted me to. (possibilidade)
  • I would do it if I were able to. (intenção condicional)
  • I might do it. (possibilidade)
  • He could have gone early, but he didn't. · He would have gone early if they had let him.
  • He might have gone early – I don't know.
  • We knew that he could / would / might be annoying. (discurso indireto)
  • Could / Would you open the door for me, please? (pedidos)
  • Could I do it for you? (ofertas)

Gerunds and infinitives

Ponto 55Gerund or infinitive?

  • Swimming is very good for you. (gerúndio como sujeito)
  • Have you finished working? (gerúndio como objeto)
  • Before leaving the house, she checked all the windows. (após preposição)
  • I hope to be there before dark. (infinitivo após verbo)
  • I got up early to do my homework. (finalidade)

Ponto 56Person + gerund

  • I have no objection to your arriving late. (formal: possessivo + gerúndio)
  • We are concerned about the company's trading in oil.
  • I don't mind him coming late. (conversacional: objeto + gerúndio)
  • We are worried about Jane working so hard.

Ponto 57to + infinitive after nouns and adjectives

  • I was pleased to see them again. · I'm surprised to hear you say that.
  • It was too hot to go out.
  • I've got some homework to do. · Is there anyone else to see?
  • Have we got enough money to go to the cinema?
  • She has a plane to catch. · There is a lot of housework to do.

Ponto 58it + to + infinitive

  • That was a stupid thing to do. · It was a strange thing to say.
  • It was nice of you to come. · It was kind of them to send me money.
  • It was good to see them again. · It was difficult to drive the car with one hand.
  • It was very kind of you to visit Janice in hospital.

Ponto 59Use of the infinitive without to

  • He made me do it. · We let them go home.
  • I saw him arrive. · We watched them go. · I felt him move.
  • I saw him arrive. / I saw him arriving. (também com present participle)
  • They made me stay at home.

Ponto 60The perfect infinitive

  • If he'd phoned, I would have met him at the airport. (3ª condicional)
  • Who could have told him the news? (modais no passado)
  • I hope to have finished this by Christmas. (após want/expect/hope)
  • I was disappointed to have missed him. (após adjetivo)

Ponto 61Gerund or infinitive? (contrast)

  • We talked about cooking. (após preposição)
  • Swimming is excellent exercise. · I can't stand going to the dentist.
  • I came here to study. (finalidade)
  • I love to visit old houses. / I love visiting old houses. (mesmo sentido)
  • I remembered to give him the money. (lembrei ANTES de dar) / I remembered giving him the money. (lembrei DEPOIS)
  • I stopped to watch the carnival. (parei PARA assistir) / I stopped watching the carnival. (parei DE assistir)
  • I tried to open the door, but it was locked. (não consegui) / I tried opening the door to let in some fresh air. (abri de fato)

Reported speech

Ponto 64Reported questions: wh questions

  • 'What's the time?' → She asked what time it was.
  • 'How's your mother?' → He asked me how my mother was.
  • She asked me how I was. / She asked how I was.
  • 'What's your name?' he asked. → He wanted to know what my name was.
  • 'How old are you?' she said. → She asked how old I was.

Ponto 65Reported questions: if / whether

  • 'Are you angry?' → He asked if I was angry.
  • 'Did you see the film?' → She asked whether I had seen the film.
  • 'Do you like Marlon Brando?' she asked. → She asked if I liked Marlon Brando.
  • 'Are you enjoying yourself?' he asked. → He asked whether I was enjoying myself.

Ponto 66Reported questions (check)

  • 'Which book did you take?' → He wanted to know which book I had taken.
  • 'Are you wearing your overcoat?' → She wanted to know if/whether I was wearing my overcoat.

Ponto 67Reported commands

  • He told the children to stop. / He told them to stop.
  • 'Don't stop!' → He told them not to stop.
  • 'Don't go!' → He told me not to go.
  • 'Sit down Mary.' → He told Mary to sit down.
  • 'Don't go near the sea, children.' → The children's mother warned them not to go near the sea.

Ponto 68Reported requests

  • 'Would you open the door, please?' → She asked me to open the door.
  • 'Could you lend me some money, please?' → He asked me to lend him some money.
  • He told me to give him some money. (ordem) vs He asked me to give him some money. (pedido)
  • 'Would you like a cigarette?' → He asked if I would like a cigarette. (oferta)
  • 'Would you pass me a cigarette?' → He asked me to pass him a cigarette. (pedido)
  • 'Would you like some coffee?' → She asked if I would like some coffee.

Ponto 70Verbs used in reporting

  • 'Oh, dear,' she said, 'I'm terribly sorry I'm late.' → She apologized for being late.
  • 'Yes, you're right. I was very foolish,' he said. → He agreed that he had been very foolish.
  • 'We're going to get married in June,' she said. → She announced that they were going to get married in June.
  • infinitivo: advise, invite, offer, promise, refuse, remind, warn.
  • gerúndio: apologise for, insist on, suggest.
  • discurso indireto: agree, announce, boast, claim, concede.

Ponto 71Reporting conversations: summarising

  • ANNE: Are you doing anything at the weekend?
  • SUE: No, we're not.
  • ANNE: Well, would you like to come and stay with us for the weekend? I know you're all in need of a rest.
  • SUE: Oh, that would be lovely! Thank you very much.
  • ANNE: Not at all, it will be nice to see you again.
  • Resumo reportado: 'Anne has invited us to stay with her for the weekend. She thought we needed a rest! I explained that we'd bought a dog and said that we could leave him with your parents, but she insisted that we should bring him.'

Sentence structure — word order

Ponto 72Direct object and indirect object

  • I've lent my book to Michael. (verbo + OD + to/for + OI)
  • I've lent Michael my book. (verbo + OI + OD)

Ponto 73Order of adverbs (manner – place – time)

  • She hit him gently. · She hit him with all her strength.
  • They work here. · I saw them at the end of the street. (lugar)
  • He left yesterday. · I saw him at four o'clock. (tempo)
  • I worked hard at the office today. (modo – lugar – tempo)
  • They ran quickly into the house. · They played noisily all afternoon.
  • Yesterday we had a really good time. · In Paris, we stayed in hotels all the time.

Ponto 74Frequency adverbs

  • Escala: never · seldom/rarely · sometimes · often · very often/frequently · always (generally/usually/normally).
  • He always left the office at five o'clock. · We sometimes saw them in the morning.
  • I often have an egg for breakfast.
  • I'll never forget him. · I've frequently seen him in here. · I don't always agree with him.
  • Do you often come here? · Frequently, I've seen him in here.

Questions and answers

Ponto 77who questions

  • Who saw you? (who = sujeito) — Someone saw you.
  • Who did you see? (who = objeto) — You saw someone.
  • Whom did you see? (whom como objeto, hoje raro)
  • What happened? · What fell off the roof? (what como sujeito)

Ponto 78Tag questions

  • They went home, didn't they? · It's hot, isn't it? (afirmativa → tag negativa)
  • He's not waiting for us, is he? · They didn't go home, did they? (negativa → tag positiva)
  • I'm going with you, aren't I? (forma irregular)
  • Jane's your friend, isn't she?
  • It's a lovely day, isn't it? · It's not very warm here, is it?
  • You're coming too, aren't you? Yes, we are.
  • They're not staying much longer, are they? No, they aren't.

Ponto 79Agreeing with tag questions

  • He's leaving soon, isn't he? → Yes, he is. (concorda) / No, he isn't. (discorda)
  • He isn't leaving today, is he? → No, he isn't. (concorda) / Yes, he is. (discorda)
  • 'You're not angry, are you?' 'No, I'm not.'
  • 'They're staying in a hotel, aren't they?' 'Yes, they are.'

Ponto 80Short answers

  • Did he say that? → Yes, he did.
  • Will you be late? → Yes, I will.
  • Has he been waiting long? → Yes, he has.
  • Do you agree? → Yes, I do.
  • Is there anything wrong? → No, there isn't.
  • Are your parents coming? → No, they aren't.
  • Isn't he coming? → No, he isn't.

Ponto 81Embedded questions

  • Where's the bank? → Could you tell me where the bank is?
  • Could you tell me what time it is, please? · Do you think this train is going to be late?
  • Where has she gone? → Could you tell me where she has gone? (NÃO: …where has she gone)
  • Could you tell me when the train leaves?
  • Do you think that it's going to rain today?

Ponto 82think, hope, expect, suppose

  • 'Do you think the train will arrive soon?' 'Yes, I think so.' / 'I hope so.' / 'I expect so.'
  • Positivo: I think/hope/expect/suppose so. · Negativo: I don't think/expect/suppose so. / I hope not.
  • She hopes so. · They don't think so. · Do you think so?
  • 'Will there be something to eat when we get there?' 'No, I don't suppose so.'

Relative clauses

Ponto 83Subject and object defining relative clauses

  • The woman who got the job has never worked in advertising before.
  • The letter that arrived this morning was wrongly addressed.
  • The man who normally works here is ill. (oração de sujeito)
  • The man who you saw yesterday is ill. (oração de objeto)

Ponto 84Contact clauses (without who/that/which)

  • She's the person who I met at the conference. → She's the person I met at the conference.
  • Have you seen the dress that I've just bought? → Have you seen the dress I've just bought?
  • The man who was feeling ill left early. (não se omite em oração de sujeito)
  • The man I shouted at didn't come back again.
  • The television I dropped never worked again.

Ponto 85whose

  • There's the man. His wallet was stolen. → There's the man whose wallet was stolen.
  • There's the lady whose dog was killed.
  • That's the man who's going to buy the company. (who's = who is)

Ponto 86Defining and non-defining relative clauses

  • 'I saw that man again.' 'Which man?' 'The man who wants to buy my house.'
  • The man who wants to buy my house is coming to see me.
  • A man, who said he knew my father, asked me for money. (não-definidora, com vírgulas)
  • I'm going to see Sheila, who I told you about yesterday.
  • I have two sisters who are living in New York at the moment. (D — pode ter mais irmãs)
  • I have two sisters, who are living in New York at the moment. (ND — só duas irmãs)

Ponto 88which referring to whole sentences

  • He turned on the television, which looked new and expensive. (which = a TV)
  • He turned on the television, which I thought was rather surprising. (which = a ação)
  • I love the countryside, which is why I want to go and live there.
  • They stayed for hours, which I was very annoyed about.

Ponto 89where, when, and why in relative clauses

  • We visited the town where I was born. (definidora)
  • I bought them at the supermarket, where I met Mrs Butler. (não-definidora)
  • I saw the film last year, when I was in Paris.
  • I think that was the time when I lost all my money.
  • There must be a reason why you said that.

Prepositions and phrasal verbs

Ponto 91Prepositions used in idioms

  • I got the job through my uncle.
  • She's been promoted. She's got two hundred people working under her now.

Ponto 92Phrasal verbs — common verbs and use

  • We need to set off early tomorrow. · Did you find out the times of the trains?
  • I requested a pay rise but they turned me down.
  • I get up at eight o'clock. · We'll pick you up outside the station. · The plane took off very quickly.
  • The car slowed down and then stopped. (= slow) · I'm going to give up smoking. (≠ give)
  • The car burned for a few minutes, then the petrol tank blew up with a loud bang.

Ponto 93Phrasal verbs that do not take an object

  • The plan fell through: we had to cancel the project.
  • She had a terrible shock when her father passed away.
  • Sem objeto: get by · pass away · sleep in · grow up · set off · take off.

Ponto 94Separable phrasal verbs that take an object

  • We picked up the children at three. / We picked the children up at three.
  • We decided to cut the tree down. / We decided to cut down the tree.
  • We decided to cut it down. (pronome ANTES da partícula; NÃO: cut down it)
  • How do you slow down the machine? / How do you slow the machine down?

Ponto 95Phrasal verbs that take an object but do not separate

  • How are we going to get round this problem? (NÃO: get this problem round)
  • She looks like her mother: don't you think she takes after her?

Ponto 96Phrasal verbs with two particles
