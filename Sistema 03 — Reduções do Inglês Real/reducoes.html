Sistema 03 — Reduções do Inglês Real

Tipo 1 — Contrações (Contractions)

As mais formais. Aceitas inclusive na escrita.

Tipo 5 — Elisões no Final de Palavras

  • -ing → -in': "What are you doing?" → "What are you doin'?"
  • -t final suprimido: "I don't know" → "I dono" / "last night" → "las' night"
  • -h inicial suprimido: "Tell him" → "Tell 'im" / "Give her" → "Give 'er"

Contextos do Dia a Dia — Exemplos Progressivos

Organizados do mais curto ao mais longo. Cada bloco mostra a forma escrita formal → forma falada natural.

Bloco A — Frases curtíssimas (1–5 palavras)

FormalFalado
I do not know.I dunno.
Give me that.Gimme that.
Let me see.Lemme see.
Come on!C'mon!
I am fine.I'm fine.
Going to go.Gonna go.
Want to try?Wanna try?

Bloco B — Frases curtas (6–10 palavras)

FormalFalado
I am going to eat now.I'm gonna eat now.
Did you see that?Didja see that?
I do not know what to do.I dunno what to do.
What do you want to eat?Whatcha wanna eat?
He has got to be kidding.He's gotta be kidding.
Could you give me a hand?Couldja gimme a hand?
I would not do that.I wouldn't do that.
She has to leave at eight.She hasta leave at eight.

Bloco C — Frases médias (11–20 palavras)

FormalFalado
I am going to the store because I have to buy some things.I'm gonna the store cuz I hafta buy some stuff.
I do not know what you are talking about.I dunno whatcha talking about.
Would you like to come with us to the park this afternoon?Wouldja like to come with us to the park this afternoon?
What are you going to do about it? There is not a lot of time.Whatcha gonna do about it? There's not a lotta time.
He has got to be somewhere around here — I just saw him a minute ago.He's gotta be somewhere around here — I just saw 'im a minute ago.

Bloco D — Situações reais

Contextos cotidianos onde as reduções aparecem de forma natural.

No café / restaurante

  • Formal: What do you want to order? I am going to have a cup of coffee.
  • Falado: Whatcha wanna order? I'm gonna have a cup a coffee.

No trabalho / reunião

  • Formal: Are you going to be at the meeting? I have got to finish this first.
  • Falado: Arya gonna be at the meeting? I gotta finish this first.

Em casa com amigos

  • Formal: Come on, let me show you something. I do not know if you have seen this before.
  • Falado: C'mon, lemme show you something. I dunno if you've seen this before.

No telefone

  • Formal: I was supposed to call her, but I do not know what to say.
  • Falado: I was s'posed to call her, but I dunno what to say.

Saindo de casa

  • Formal: Give me a second — I have to get my keys. Do you want to drive or should I?
  • Falado: Gimme a second — I hafta get my keys. Wanna drive or should I?

Por que isso importa para brasileiros?

O português tem muito menos elisão sistemática na fala formal. O brasileiro tende a pronunciar cada sílaba com clareza, o que:

  1. Soa artificial em inglês coloquial.
  2. Dificulta a compreensão oral, porque o nativo fala com todas essas reduções e o ouvido brasileiro não reconhece as palavras.
  3. Cria uma distância perceptual: o aluno aprende "going to" mas ouve "gonna" e não conecta os dois.
