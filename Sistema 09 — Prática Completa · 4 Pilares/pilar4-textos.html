BLOCO A — TRABALHO (textos 1–25)

TEXTO 01 — "Monday Morning" | Nível: Iniciante
Tom wakes up at 7am. He is tired.
He goes to the kitchen and makes coffee.
The coffee is hot. Tom drinks it slowly.
He looks at the window. It is raining outside.
"Not a great Monday," he thinks.
He gets his bag and goes to work anyway.
VOCABULÁRIO: wakes up · tired · kitchen · slowly · raining · anyway
PERGUNTA: Why does Tom think it's not a great Monday?
CENA: personagem na cozinha, chuva na janela, xícara fumegando

TEXTO 02 — "The Email" | Nível: Iniciante
Sarah sits at her desk. She opens her computer.
She has 47 new emails. She sighs.
"Too many emails," she says.
She starts with the most important one.
It's from her boss. He wants a report by noon.
Sarah looks at the clock. It's 10am. She has two hours.
"I can do this," she says. She starts writing.
VOCABULÁRIO: desk · opens · sighs · important · report · noon · clock
PERGUNTA: How much time does Sarah have to write the report?
CENA: personagem no computador, caixa de entrada cheia, relógio

TEXTO 03 — "The Meeting" | Nível: Iniciante
The meeting starts at 2pm. Everyone sits down.
The manager speaks first. He talks for a long time.
Some people are bored. One man falls asleep.
Then someone asks a question. The room wakes up.
At 3:30, the meeting ends. People are relieved.
"Finally!" says one woman. They all laugh.
VOCABULÁRIO: meeting · manager · bored · falls asleep · relieved · finally
PERGUNTA: What time does the meeting end?
CENA: sala de reunião, pessoas em volta da mesa, um adormecido

TEXTO 04 — "Lunch Break" | Nível: Iniciante
It's noon. Time for lunch.
Carlos goes to the restaurant near his office.
He orders a sandwich and a juice.
He sits alone and checks his phone.
He has a message from his wife: "How's your day?"
He smiles and writes back: "Busy. But okay."
He finishes his lunch and goes back to work.
VOCABULÁRIO: noon · orders · sandwich · checks · message · smiles · writes back
PERGUNTA: Who sends Carlos a message?
CENA: restaurante simples, personagem sozinho com sanduíche e celular

TEXTO 05 — "Working Late" | Nível: Intermediário

She thinks about going home, but the work isn't done.
She takes a deep breath and keeps typing.
An hour later, she finishes. She packs her bag.
Walking out, she feels exhausted but proud.
VOCABULÁRIO: deadline · project · exhausted · proud · packs · keeps typing · deep breath

The interviewer comes in. She shakes his hand firmly.
"Tell me about yourself," she says.
David takes a breath and begins. He prepared for this.
The interview goes well. At the end, she smiles.
"We'll be in touch," she says. He leaves feeling hopeful.
VOCABULÁRIO: nervous · arrives · shaking · interviewer · firmly · prepared · hopeful · be in touch
PERGUNTA: Why does David feel hopeful at the end?

They all react with laughing emojis.

TEXTO 08 — "The Promotion" | Nível: Intermediário
Mark has been at this company for four years.

Today, his manager calls him into the office.
Mark's heart beats faster. Did he do something wrong?
"Mark," says the manager, "we're promoting you."
Mark blinks. He didn't expect this. "Thank you," he says.
Walking back to his desk, he can't stop smiling.
He calls his wife. "You won't believe what just happened."
VOCABULÁRIO: promoting · blinks · expect · deadline · beating · believe
PERGUNTA: How did Mark feel when his manager called him in?

Linda was furious, but she said nothing in the meeting.
Later, she went to her manager and explained everything.
The manager nodded. "I know," he said. "I've noticed."
Linda felt better. Sometimes patience pays off.
VOCABULÁRIO: credit · presented · furious · explained · nodded · noticed · patience · pays off
PERGUNTA: What does Paulo do that is unfair?

TEXTO 10 — "End of the Year" | Nível: Avançado
It was the last working day of the year.
The team gathered in the conference room.
Someone brought a cake. Another brought sparkling water.
The manager made a short speech about the year's challenges.
He mentioned the project that almost failed — and then didn't.
He talked about the new clients, the long nights, the setbacks.
"But we made it," he said. "Together."
Everyone raised their glasses. Someone started clapping.
It wasn't a perfect year. But it was theirs.
VOCABULÁRIO: gathered · sparkling · speech · challenges · setbacks · raised · clapping · theirs
PERGUNTA: What does the manager say about the difficult project?
